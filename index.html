About

            Diagnosing whether a fetus has brain abnormalities during pregnancy is increasingly crucial. With higher education levels, elevated expectations for children, and a growing trend of delayed parenthood, accurate prenatal diagnosis becomes paramount, for all the reasons above. Brain abnormalities in infants are often assessed through ultrasound, examining conditions such as arachnoid cysts, cisterna magna, etc. These brain abnormalities mostly occur in infants after birth, which can significantly impact infant survival rates, with severe cases resulting in non viable pregnancies or low survival rates. Although prenatal brain abnormalities are relatively common, many pregnancies progress to full term, especially when the abnormalities are not severe. Medical evidence regarding the causes of these abnormalities is often unclear. Accurate prenatal diagnosis is of utmost importance, particularly for this brain abnormality, which is rank among the most among the most frequent reasons for pregnancy termination.

            Currently, technological advancements have made significant strides, and the development of artificial intelligence systems to aid in screening fetal brain abnormalities is crucial. This plays a vital role in diagnosing and caring for the health of both the fetus and the mother from the prenatal to postnatal stages. The system utilizes health examination data from the mother and fetus, including brain ultrasound images, facilitating swift and accurate disease diagnosis. This enables prompt and more efficient treatment and care for both the infant and the mother. Additionally, the system alleviates the workload of medical professionals by efficiently analyzing data and making medical decisions. This proves highly beneficial for the treatment and healthcare of both the fetus and the mother during the prenatal period.
